Understanding Static vs Dynamic QR Codes

Two types of QR codes, built for different needs

What Is a Static QR Code?

A static QR code encodes the destination URL directly into the pattern. Your device reads the URL from the image and opens it — no server needed, works offline. The catch: once created, the destination is locked in. If the URL changes, you need a new QR code.

What Is a Dynamic QR Code?

A dynamic QR code encodes a short redirect URL managed by a platform like QR Cheetah. You control where the redirect points from your dashboard, so you can change the destination anytime without touching the printed QR code.

When to Use Which

Static is fine for personal links or permanent URLs. Dynamic is better for business — when URLs change, campaigns rotate, or you need scan data.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about static and dynamic QR codes

What is a dynamic QR code?

A dynamic QR code uses a short redirect URL instead of encoding the destination directly. You can change where it points anytime — even after printing — and it tracks scan analytics (location, device, frequency).

What is a static QR code?

A static QR code embeds the URL directly into the pattern. It works offline with no server needed, but the destination is permanent — if the URL changes, you need a new code.

Can I edit a QR code after printing?

Only with a dynamic QR code. The printed image stays the same — you change where the redirect points from your QR Cheetah dashboard.

Do dynamic QR codes expire?

No. Dynamic QR codes on QR Cheetah never expire — free or paid. The redirect stays active indefinitely.

Is the static QR code generator on this page really free?

Yes. It runs in your browser — no data sent to any server, no account needed, no limits.

What happens if my destination URL stops working?

With a static code, every printed copy points to a dead link. With a dynamic code, you log in and redirect it to a working URL.

Can I add a logo to my QR code?

Yes. QR Cheetah lets you upload a logo, customize colors, dot shapes, and corner patterns. High error correction keeps the code scannable with a logo overlay.

How do I shorten a QR code URL?

Dynamic codes already use a short redirect URL, so the pattern is simpler. For static codes, shorten your URL first with URL Unicorn, then generate the QR code.
